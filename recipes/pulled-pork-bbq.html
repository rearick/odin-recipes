Ingredients

  • 1 bone in pork butt. 8-10 lbs.
  • Meat Church Holy Voodoo (sub Honey Hog, Honey Bacon, Gospel Holy Gospel or Deez Nuts)
  • 1 T, yellow mustard for slather

Instructions

  1. Prepare your Smoker

    Prepare your smoker at a temperature of 200 degrees. I recommend a medium smoke wood for this cook such as hickory or pecan. We used a Traeger Timberline XL with Meat Church pellets which is a blend of oak and hickory in this video.

  2. Pork butt prep

    Remove all excess fat from the pork butt only leaving minimal fat on the bottom, although this is not that important as you can remove it after the cook if you desire. In this video we removed all the fat so we could build maximum bark. In other methods I often just score the fat. The choice is yours.

  3. Season the pork butt

    Slather the pork butt in yellow mustard to act as a binder for the seasoning. This will not affect the flavor. Rather, it allows the seasoning to adhere more quickly. Season all sides of the pork liberally with Holy Voodoo. Allow the pork butt to allow let the seasoning fully adhere. Allow at least 30 minutes but you can also do that over night.

  4. Smoke the pork butt

    This low and slow cook should take around 12 hours or so depending on weight

    1. Place the pork butt in the smoker

    2. Smoke until 175-180 internal

      You can spritz occasionally with cider vinegar if you like, but in this video we left for the day and didn't do a thing to the pork while it cooked. It took 10 hours for our pork butt to reach 180 internal.

    3. Increase the temp of your smoker to 275 (+ or - to your preference)

      Continue smoking until the pork butt is probe tender which should be just over 200 internal temperature.

  5. Pull the pork butt

    1. Remove the pork butt from the smoker and allow to rest

      The haters will say it's burned. It's not.

    2. Pull the pork
    3. Add more seasoning of choice to enchance the flavor
    4. Add sauce if you like

  6. Enjoy!
